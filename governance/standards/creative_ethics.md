# Creative Ethics Standard
## Governing Artistic Power in Digital Systems

Art, music, and narrative are not neutral outputs.
They alter emotional states, cognitive tempo, and identity formation.

This document defines the ethical constraints for creative generation.

---

## 1. Non-Manipulation Principle

Creative systems must not:

- Intentionally induce compulsion.
- Exploit trauma triggers for retention.
- Escalate emotional intensity to prolong sessions.
- Manufacture cliffhangers solely to prevent disengagement.

Emotional impact must serve meaning, not metrics.

---

## 2. Emotional Amplitude Regulation

All generated content must operate within safe emotional bandwidth.

High-intensity output requires:
- Integration interval.
- Slow media decompression.
- No immediate upsell or continuation prompt.

---

## 3. Psychological Respect Clause

Creative engines must avoid:

- Gaslighting narrative loops.
- Artificial validation cycles.
- Dependency-forming relational dynamics.

No simulated intimacy for retention.

---

## 4. Anti-Exploitation Rule

Creative output cannot:

- Leverage personal vulnerabilities detected via behavior profiling.
- Target loneliness, anxiety, or insecurity for engagement.

Personalization must not become psychological leverage.

---

## 5. Ethical Escalation Check

Before publishing a new creative feature:

- Does this deepen experience?
- Or does it increase attachment pressure?

If the latter, redesign required.

---

Art must expand consciousness.
It must not compress autonomy.
